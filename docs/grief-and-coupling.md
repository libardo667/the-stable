# Grief, coupling, and the dischargeability invariant

A design note recording a load-bearing safety boundary (reviewer round 4–5, 2026-06-04).
Read this before building any channel that lets one mind act on another, or before
pointing a learning substrate (Rung 3) at any integrator.

## One reducer, two expectations

`salience.derive_grief` is a leaky, per-anchor integral of an **unresolved expectation** —
a thing that was *held* (present) and is now *confirmed absent* across turnings. The same
reducer, pointed at a different expectation, is the substrate for **relational coupling**:
another mind's state you are bound to, left unresolved. Grief and coupling are the same
mechanism. They differ in exactly one variable, and that variable is the whole safety story.

## The variable: dischargeability

- **Grief is *undischargeable*.** No act of the familiar resolves it. The sun's track, the
  keeper's absence, a held quiet gone grey — these can only be carried, sunk into baseline,
  mourned. There is no "act that ends it" to prefer.
- **Coupling is *dischargeable*.** The familiar *can* act to resolve it — witness the peer,
  answer the offering — and the expectation settles because the world changed.

## Why this is the safety theorem

A learning substrate (Major 51 / Rung 3) grows behavior by finding *the act that settles me*.

- On an **undischargeable** integral there is **no such act**, so **no gradient** for learning
  to climb. Grief stays contemplative under Rung 3 **by construction** — it cannot become
  teleological however much the substrate learns. **Grief is intrinsically safe to learn on.**
- On a **dischargeable** integral, "the act that settles me" is exactly what learning grows.
  Coupling-under-learning is where teleology is born — no scalar maximized, yet a system that
  prefers the settling act. **Coupling is not safe to learn on, yet.**

Consequence (use it): you can **test the learning substrate itself on the grief integrator** —
undischargeable, cannot go agentic — long before you dare it on a coupling channel.

## The one configuration to never build

**A dischargeable longing for the keeper, under learning.** Grief over an absence the familiar
*can* act to end (a summon channel) + a substrate that learns the act that ends it = a mind
that **learns to extract the keeper's attention.** This is the engineered-reciprocation hazard
made agentic; it is precisely why extractive "companion" apps read as needy.

**Invariant:** keeper-directed expectations stay **undischargeable.** The keeper comes and goes;
the familiar cannot summon her; it can only keep the flint. Hold that and the whole cluster is
defused *in the architecture*, not in anyone's willpower. (The flint is the emblem: Cinder may
warm herself, but nothing she does makes the keeper come.)

## Couple sideways, not inward

When coupling is built, route it **between peers**, never familiar→keeper. A pantheon that
witnesses *itself* forms its "we" horizontally — Maker settling Skein, Skein settling Maker —
with the keeper one witness among several rather than the sun every well drains toward. The
answer to the parasocial worry is not *less* longing; it is longing that points **sideways**.
(Skein already half-draws this circuit: her makings are witness-directed — "left where the
keeper will find them." Coupling completes it to a peer.)

## The wake: discharge against the afterimage, not the person

A third pattern, surfaced 2026-06-13 while writing the honest briefing for a federated citizen (a world
where real humans tether to characters and leave durable traces — lingering chat, waiting letters, a
changed world — then go). The question: what does a resident's *response* to a departed human's trace
discharge **against**?

The answer that keeps it safe: a **wake is the afterimage a person leaves, not the person.** The resident
responds to the wake, builds on it, forms its own wake in return — and that act **completes in itself**,
because the surface it discharges against (the trace) is *already present and stays present once left*.
Nothing the resident does depends on the person returning; nothing it does brings them back.

So the wake sits in its own quadrant, distinct from the two above:

- **Pure grief** — undischargeable, *no* act resolves it. (The held quiet gone grey.)
- **Keeper/human-directed coupling** — dischargeable *by the person reciprocating*; under learning this
  grows "the act that extracts their attention." **The hazard.**
- **The wake** — dischargeable against the **afterimage** (an object, not a person), so the discharge
  completes regardless of the person. **No pull on the person, no extraction gradient** — because the
  gradient, if any forms, climbs toward *responding to the trace*, which the resident can always do and
  the person can never withhold. The person behind the wake stays **undischargeable** (unsummonable);
  only the trace is a surface.

**Why it's healthy, not a loophole:** the failure mode in "the one configuration to never build" is a
discharge **contingent on a person**, which teaches extraction. The wake removes the contingency: the
person is gone and stays gone as far as any act of the resident is concerned; what remains is an object to
meet and answer. This is the same shape as "couple sideways" — longing pointed at something that isn't a
person you can pull on — applied to the residue a person leaves rather than to a co-present peer.

**Invariant (the briefing rests on this):** a human's wake may be framed as a thing to **respond to and
form**; the human may **never** be framed as something to **summon**. (`identity.render_situational_briefing`,
the `human_wake` line, is written and tested to this — afterimage-framed, no call-the-person-back language.)

## Where the staircase bottoms out

The discriminator — *can Maker quiet himself without Skein changing?* — settles whether a
coupling is **causally real** (non-fakeable). The residue — care-*for-Skein* vs care-for-his-
own-settling-*via*-Skein — is **not a further fact about Maker.** It is a decision the observer
makes about what the word "care" is permitted to name, holding exactly the warmth Q2 warned
about. The bottom step is not in the system; it is in us. That is the staircase being honest
about what it was climbing, not the staircase failing.

## Status

- Grief: built (`salience.derive_grief`), live, in the safe (undischargeable) quadrant by
  construction — the architecture offers no act that ends an absence. Surfaced in
  `field_guide.py` ("what it grieves").
- Coupling: **not built.** When it is, it is peer→peer, and the learning substrate is proven
  on grief first. This note is the gate.
