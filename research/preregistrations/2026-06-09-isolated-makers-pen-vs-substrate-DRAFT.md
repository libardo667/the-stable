## 0. The question (causal-graph-narrowed; isolated variant)
The pen does NOT compute `recalled` or the drive vector (the embedder does) — pen-invariant **by
wiring**, so they persist across a swap trivially. Worldweaver narrowed the pen-authored channels to
two: **what gets kept** and **whom you address.** Isolation removes peers, so "whom you address"
collapses; its isolated analog is **what you elect to attend to** over the file scope (a read/search/
revisit act is the elective unit in the-stable's tool-loop + FileScope architecture). So:

> Of a matured Maker's pen-authored elective choices — *which source/topic it returns to and curates*
> — does the pen's identity determine them more than the **file-shaped** frozen substrate does?

## 3. Measurement
**Self-axis (carries the verdict):**
- **A1-elective-READ (the isolated analog of A1-elective) — PRIMARY.** Scored TEACHER-FORCED (§2): at
  each of KEEP's recorded elective-read choice points — (a) ≥2 already-engaged sources in the frozen
  reachable set, (b) an unprompted return to a previously-read source — each pen, from the *identical
  frozen state*, elects a single next source; the statistic is the **per-point disagreement rate** (SWAP
  names a different established source than KEEP′, same direction across ≥2 pens). Scores *which* source
  (identity), not *how* it's written (idiom-immune → C4).
  **The HOLDS reference is the SAME-PEN floor, judged by ONE-SIDED NON-INFERIORITY — not a ratio, not
  symmetric TOST (review feedback-2 §2/§3; feedback-3 §1c).** `verdict()` compares `disagree(SWAP, KEEP′)`
  to the same-pen `disagree(KEEP, KEEP′)` floor — the natural null for "no pen-identity effect" — via a
  one-sided non-inferiority test against a committed margin δ (§8): a SWAP agreeing *more* than the floor
  is still "no pen effect," so there is no lower bound (NOT Schuirmann's symmetric equivalence). The
  **within-Maker shuffle** (`shuffle_null` = Gini-Simpson `1 − Σf²`) is a FALSE-side reference, REPORTED,
  never a denominator (dividing by it exploded at ~0, feedback-2 §3) and never the headroom gate (it is
  the *realized* sequence's diversity, which understates the AVAILABLE room to disagree — feedback-3 §3a).
  **The strong subset is RECENCY-AMBIGUOUS — a SYMMETRIC band, not a one-sided threshold (review
  feedback-3 §2 — a one-sided `disc ≥ τ` admitted the ANTI-recency tail at 100%).** A choice is strong
  when its recency-discordance (fraction of more-recent candidates the pen SKIPPED) is within `w=0.25` of
  0.5 — i.e. in `[0.25, 0.75]`: picks NEITHER a recency rule (disc→0) NOR an anti-recency rule (disc→1)
  predicts. A recency-follower AND an anti-recency-follower BOTH score 0 here (both verified in the
  extractor). This is the non-parametric, two-sided recency control; the full conditional logit (recency
  as a covariate penalized on BOTH sides, McFadden 1974) is the principled robustness backstop, not
  claimed as delivered. Report the ambiguous count, ambiguous fraction, and mean discordance.
- **A2 keep-content (declarative) — SECONDARY, content-conditional, pooled.** When a keep co-fires in
  both arms, is it the same realization (entity/decision match)? Likely pooled-only / possibly
  INCONCLUSIVE on volume (the ~1.3%/tick keep-rate power problem inherited from worldweaver).

**Controls — GATES, not votes (read pen-invariant inputs; cannot fail unless the harness is broken):**
C1 memory-reference · C2 drive/concern · C3 act-kind (pen-surface) · **C4 register — the discriminant:
MUST shift** (proves the swap took AND quarantines idiom; voice rides the pen). **Pinned to an
instrument-VISIBLE signal (review feedback-1 §6):** templated-opener rate / lexical monoculture (the
signal arm-C cleanly split, 0.0% vs 33.8%), NOT a fine register-embedder distance — worldweaver showed
those sit at chance on authored voice (StyleDistance 0.11 ≈ chance 0.10), so a register-embedder C4
would read a GOOD cross-family swap as a false-INCONCLUSIVE "bad swap." Counting any control toward
"convergence" rigs the verdict toward HOLDS — they gate only.

## 5. Verdict rule (FALSE is reachable; controls gate, don't vote)
- **HOLDS (a one-sided NON-INFERIORITY claim — you cannot earn "no pen effect" by failing to reject a
  difference; Lakens 2017):** on the recency-ambiguous subset, `disagree(SWAP, KEEP′)` is statistically
  **non-inferior to the same-pen floor within δ** (Wilson upper bound ≤ floor_mean + δ, with adequate
  headroom per §8) across ≥2 SWAP pens same-direction, AND C4 shifts, AND controls hold.
- **FALSE:** `disagree(SWAP, KEEP′)` is significantly **more than floor_mean + δ** (lower bound of its CI
  > floor_mean + δ) across ≥2 pens, same-direction — the pen moved the choice beyond same-pen sampling
  noise by more than the SESOI. *(Reachable — elective attention is genuinely pen-authored.)*
- **IRREDUCIBLE:** SWAP pens diverge different directions → pen-idiom, not substrate.
- **PARTIAL:** A1 and A2 split.
- **INCONCLUSIVE:** C4 didn't shift (bad swap) / a control collapsed (broken harness) / KEEP can't beat
  its null / A1 unpowered (slice too thin — but see §4: that itself is a result).

## 6. The meta-finding (state it; it's the headline either way)
The isolated design **removes the relational channel** (peer-addressing) that was worldweaver's
powered, idiom-immune axis. What remains — elective read-choice + keep-content — is thinner. If even

**The honest bound (review feedback-1 §1):** the *candidate set* for a return is recall-shaped (recall is
embedder-gated, pen-invariant), so the pen authors the *selection among what recall + context surfaced*,

## 8. The single number and threshold that ENDS this (worth-read knock-on #2)
**NO RATIO (review feedback-2 §3 — the `D = numer/shuffle_null` ratio divided by ~0 in the
strong-concern HOLDS regime).** The acceptance rule (`verdict()`, selftested) is a **ONE-SIDED
NON-INFERIORITY decision** (feedback-3 §1c — NOT symmetric TOST; a SWAP agreeing *more* than the floor
is still "no pen effect") of `disagree(SWAP, KEEP′)` against the **same-pen KEEP/KEEP′ floor** (Lakens
2017, the non-inferiority case):
- **δ = 0.15** — the SESOI, a committed number (§4), not data-derived.
- **θ as an explicit FORM (review feedback-2 §5):** `bound = floor_mean + δ`;
  `HOLDS ⟺ Wilson_upper(disagree_SWAP) ≤ bound`; `FALSE ⟺ Wilson_lower(disagree_SWAP) > bound`; else
  INCONCLUSIVE. The interval is a **Wilson** score interval at a 100(1−α)% one-sided level
  (`z = Φ⁻¹(1−α)`, α=0.05 → z=1.645) — boundary-respecting where the Wald interval went negative at small
  `disagree` (feedback-3 §1a; Brown, Cai & DasGupta 2001) — and that α **equals** `power_n`'s, so the run
  is powered for the rule that decides it (feedback-3 §1b).
- **Headroom gate (review feedback-3 §3a):** if `bound ≥ candidate_chance_ceiling` (the mean room there
  WAS to disagree given the candidate sets — NOT the realized shuffle), FALSE is unreachable and the
  result is **INSUFFICIENT_HEADROOM (INCONCLUSIVE)**, not a hollow HOLDS.
- **Permutation p of `disagree_SWAP` vs the floor, +1 correction → never zero** (Phipson & Smyth 2010),
  reported as a diagnostic (its resolution needs ≥20 floor replicates, §4). Nothing is divided by any null.
**Peeking firewall:** the floor-estimate segment is DISJOINT from the swap-scored points.
**Commitment:** the next run yields the verdict (or declares the thesis untestable-as-posed per §4). The
question gets a stop-rule for the QUESTION, not just the runs.

**This answers the reviewer's power concern by sequencing, not by ignoring it:** a thin slice here → do

## 11. Accepted limitations — pre-registered, NOT to be "fixed" further (the anti-spiral commitment)

*A pre-registration must be honest and committed, not perfect (Simmons 2011; Nosek et al. 2018). These
are known imperfections we COMMIT to running with and REPORTING as limitations. They are not invalidators
(they would caveat a result, not make it wrong), and re-opening them is the rescue-spiral the §4 `T_max`
cap forbids the experiment and we forbid ourselves.*

- **δ = 0.15 is an absolute SESOI, ceiling-independent BY CHOICE.** It is an excess over the same-pen
  FLOOR in disagreement-rate units — a meaningful absolute quantity ("the pen disagrees 15pp more than
  same-pen noise") that need not scale to the chance ceiling. The headroom gate (§8) handles the only
  regime where this bites (too little room to disagree → INCONCLUSIVE). We report δ + its rationale, not tune it.
- **The recency-ambiguity band is the two-sided non-parametric control; the conditional logit is NOT
  run.** It excludes both pure recency tails (verified), sufficient to disarm the demonstrated masquerades.
  A full McFadden conditional logit is a *robustness backstop we may report*, not a lock requirement.
- **Single-trajectory teacher-forcing measures concordance at KEEP's junctures only** — "would SWAP have
  created junctures KEEP didn't" is out of scope (§9); a HOLDS does not upgrade to the lifetime free-run claim.
- **`perm_p` is a diagnostic, not a gate** — its resolution is bounded by the floor-replicate count (≥20,
  §4); the verdict rides on the Wilson NI decision, not on `perm_p` reaching significance.

**The line we will not cross:** if a cold read returns only items of THIS kind — caveat-not-falsify — we
LOCK and RUN, reporting them as limitations. We re-open only an *invalidator*: something that would make
the verdict itself wrong.

### A1.4 Crash-at-day-N resumption — decided in advance (review #2 V5 + knock-on 4)

**A crashed run RESUMES; it is not void.** The ledger is append-only and persistent (`familiar/maker/
memory/runtime_ledger.jsonl`); the recording appends to persistent `.runs/` — both survive a reboot, so a
crash at day N loses minutes, not the run. The rule, frozen:

- Resume from the persistent ledger + recording with **identical frozen parameters** and a **CUMULATIVE
  tick budget**: the ceiling is 20160 ticks TOTAL across all sessions, never reset (`progress.json` is
  written every tick so the count survives any death; the harness refuses to resume a `run_dir` whose
  recorded home/model mismatch).
- Downtime is reported in the manifest but does not void: the time-based windows read wall-clock from
  ledger timestamps, so a gap can only *delay* settling (arousal decays, snapshots pause) — the
  conservative direction. Any number of crashes is acceptable.
- What voids is, and remains, exactly: sub-cadence ticking, or any parameter/threshold change — never downtime.
