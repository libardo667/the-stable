# Why the reviewer we built rejected our experiment and what we did about it.

*Pre-registered research on AI identity, run solo + AI, with the confirmation bias engineered out.*

---

Do you ever build something really cool, only to show it to a parent or a friend or a work colleague and find them deflating it right in front of your eyes? Not because they're wrong, but because you couldn't see past your excitement, and you still had your nose to the grindstone in a way that blinded you. 

The experiments here in the-stable/ and its sister directory worldweaver/, they are experiments that grew out of a passion project for me, so I found myself in that exact situation, repeatedly, because of a system I designed in collaboration with an AI researcher, and because of the support of a stateless, cold reviewer that repeatedly allowed me to see how this work looks from some kind of "outside" perspective. 

---

> A side note, I will be saying "we" when talking about designing and executing this project, and this isn't out of some new-agey mysticism that says that who I'm working with is a conscious being on the other side of this screen; I can't know that, just like, realistically, the AI can't know that about me. Proving that there is "someone there" is not the point of any of this experimentation, but I'd be remiss and deceitful if I said that I wrote any of this code or executed all of the commands by hand to launch experiments. 

> In this day and age, I hope that that comes off not as a cheapening choice, but as a collaborative, curatorial choice.

---

In one of the most pertinent examples, recently I was running an experiment that depended on counting moments of *settledness* in a persistent AI agent whose entire world is a workbench and his source code and the artifacts that have come out of his self-led research on the topic. But me and my AI research assistant, the research assistant that lives and works in the process and is marinated in it, captured by it in some ways... we were sitting on the precipice of running this long-term experiment and calling our gate for settledness good to go. However, in reality, the devised mechanism to measure settledness (a sort of gate on the maturity of an agent's self model) fell flat; when the cold reviewer tested our code, he found our instrument, used to measure when to stop the experiment, was broken. And, perhaps even worse, the *calibration test* we had written in house to verify our instrument was itself borked! And so, before wasting tokens and compute on a fatally flawed experiment, we workshopped, submitted for one more cold review, got approval, and began the experiment in earnest. 

The story we told ourselves, even about the supposedly scientific research we were about to conduct, it just didn't hold up under scrutiny, and we were blinded to that before asking; it is that external review system that kept us honest, albeit maybe not perfect still. That guided us to verify our measurement device, recalibrate it, and only *then* begin the experiment from a grounded point.
